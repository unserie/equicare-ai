Methodology

Audit pipeline

Three-stage pipeline: metric computation across protected attribute strata, SHAP-based proxy variable attribution, and ethics-framework-anchored mitigation synthesis via RAG over Belmont Report, WHO AI Ethics (2021), and key algorithmic fairness literature.

01
System characterisation
Define the deployed decision task (classification, risk scoring, ranking), model architecture, protected attribute set including intersectional combinations, and deployment context — population, decision stakes, HITL configuration.
Task: binary classification · Architecture: XGBoost · Protected: race × SES · n=45,715 · Stakes: high
02
Stratified fairness metrics
Compute DIR, equalized odds (TPR and FPR parity), calibration error, and individual fairness score across all strata with bootstrapped 95% CIs. SHAP attribution identifies proxy variables via P(sensitive | feature) > threshold.
DIR: 0.61 · ΔFPRrace: 21.4pp [18.1, 24.7] · Calibration gap: ±0.09 · Top proxy: prior_arrests (SHAP: 0.38)
03
Mitigation synthesis
RAG-generated mitigation strategies mapped to technical interventions: threshold optimisation (Hardt et al., 2016), preprocessing reweighting (Kamiran & Calders, 2012), adversarial debiasing (Zhang et al., 2018), post-processing calibration (Pleiss et al., 2017).
Primary: threshold recalibration per stratum → DIR: 0.61 → 0.84 · Secondary: reweighting → EO gap: 21.4pp → 6.2pp
Conceptual Framework

Mechanisms of algorithmic bias

Algorithmic bias operates through distinct mechanisms that differ structurally from classical statistical bias.

01
Confirmed
Proxy variable propagation
Prior_utilisation → health_cost → race (r=0.68)
SHAP attribution: 0.34 · DIR: 0.61
›mechanism detail
A feature f with high predictive utility encodes a protected attribute a via a learned conditional P(a|f) > threshold, producing disparate impact without explicit inclusion of a. Detectable via SHAP attribution and conditional independence testing. Not removable by feature deletion if f is a linear combination of correlated features.
02
Confirmed
Label noise & measurement bias
Y* = recidivism · Ŷ = re-arrest
P(Ŷ=1|Y*=0, Black) ≠ P(Ŷ=1|Y*=0, White)
›mechanism detail
Ground truth labels are proxies for the true outcome subject to differential measurement error across groups. Re-arrest as a proxy for recidivism inherits the differential policing intensity signal. Healthcare cost as a proxy for health need inherits historical access barriers. The model learns the measurement error as signal.
03
Suspected
Distributional shift & feedback loops
P(X_t+1 | M_t) ≠ P(X_t) → covariate shift
Re-audit cadence: ≤ quarterly
›mechanism detail
A deployed model alters the data-generating process it was trained on (D'Amour et al., 2020). Predictive policing increases arrest rates in targeted areas, validating the model's predictions. Fairness degrades monotonically under these dynamics without scheduled re-auditing.
04
Formally proved
Fairness metric incompatibility
Calibration + FPR parity + FNR parity
→ mutually incompatible when P(Y=1|A=0) ≠ P(Y=1|A=1)
›mechanism detail
Chouldechova (2017) and Kleinberg et al. (2016) formally prove that calibration, equal FPR, and equal FNR cannot simultaneously hold when base rates differ across groups. There is no universally fair algorithm — the choice of fairness criterion is a normative, domain-specific decision, not a technical one.

Key literature — hover author line for DOI · click to expand
›
Barocas, Hardt & Narayanan (2023)Review
Fairness and Machine Learning: Limitations and Opportunities. MIT Press. Open access at fairmlbook.org. The platform's fairness criterion taxonomy and metric definitions derive directly from this work.
›
Chouldechova (2017)Formal proof
Big Data 5(2):153–163, 2017. DOI: 10.1089/big.2016.0047. Foundation for the FPR/FNR asymmetry documented in COMPAS. Cited in every panel where error rate parity and calibration are evaluated together.
›
Obermeyer et al. (2019)Empirical
Science 366:447–453, 2019. DOI: 10.1126/science.aax2342. The Optum scenario is a direct replication of this finding. At equivalent algorithmic risk scores, Black patients had significantly greater chronic condition burden — the algorithm systematically under-referred them to care management.
›
Hardt, Price & Srebro (2016)Empirical
NeurIPS 2016. Implemented in Fairlearn as ThresholdOptimizer. Estimated ΔFPRrace: 21.4pp → 6.2pp for COMPAS at ~1.8% accuracy cost. Platform classifies as Low effort / High impact.
›
Dwork et al. (2012)Formal proof
ITCS 2012. DOI: 10.1145/2090236.2090255. The individual fairness score in the Audit Metrics radar operationalises the Lipschitz constraint. Selection of the similarity metric is domain-specific and normatively loaded.
›
Kamiran & Calders (2012)Empirical
KAIS 33(1), 2012. Implemented in AIF360 as Reweighing. Estimated ΔDIR: 0.61 → 0.79; ΔFPRrace: 21.4pp → 8.2pp for COMPAS. Platform classifies as Medium effort / Highest impact.
›
Zhang, Lemoine & Mitchell (2018)Empirical
AIES 2018. Implemented in AIF360 as AdversarialDebiasing. Estimated ΔDIR: 0.61 → 0.74. High effort (full retraining pipeline required). Platform classifies as Medium impact.
›
D'Amour et al. (2020)Empirical
FAccT 2020. Motivates the platform's quarterly re-audit cadence recommendation. The FAIR 'Reusable' dimension directly operationalises this finding. All five landmark scenarios are flagged for absent re-audit schedules.
›
Dressel & Farid (2018)Empirical
Science Advances 4(1):eaao5580, 2018. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.aao5580. Cited in the Technical Summary accordion block as evidence that COMPAS's discriminative value does not justify its documented disparate impact.
›
Hoppe et al. (2019)Empirical
Science Advances 5(10):eaaw7238, 2019. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.aaw7238. The NIH scenario is based on this study. Patient/community-oriented research — disproportionately selected by Black applicants — receives lower priority scores from review panels, contributing to but not fully explaining the funding gap.
›
Pleiss et al. (2017)Empirical
NeurIPS 2017. Applied per-group via sklearn CalibratedClassifierCV. Trades FPR/FNR parity for calibration parity subject to Chouldechova (2017). Platform classifies as Low effort / Medium impact (+7 pts equity score).
›
Kearns et al. (2018)Formal proof
ICML 2018. Motivates the platform's intersectional audit tags. Fairness guaranteed for marginal groups can fail for intersectional subgroups (Race × Gender, Race × SES). All five landmark scenarios are flagged for absent intersectional auditing.
